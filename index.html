Case Study: Confidence Through Structured Goal Setting

The Challenge

Middle school PE students often disengage when they perceive themselves as "not athletic." Research in sport psychology (specifically Bandura's Self-Efficacy Theory and Locke & Latham's Goal Setting Theory) suggests that confidence is built through structured success experiences, not innate talent. How do you design a learning experience that teaches students to build their own confidence?

Design Process

D
Design
D
Develop
I
Implement
E
Evaluate

Analysis

Learner Profile: Grades 6-8, mixed ability levels, many with low physical self-concept. Students often set vague goals ("get better") or avoid challenge entirely.

Needs Assessment: Students lacked a framework for translating effort into progress. Without measurable targets, they couldn't recognize improvement, leading to disengagement.

Standards Alignment: SHAPE America Standards 4 & 5, CASEL SEL Competencies (Self-Awareness, Self-Management), Illinois State Goals 19, 20, 21.

Design Decisions

  • Scaffolded 5-lesson structure: each lesson builds on the previous, moving from understanding confidence to applying it
  • Interactive format: knowledge checks, reflection prompts, slider activities, scenario-based learning, and a goal-building tool
  • Grounded in research: Bandura's four sources of self-efficacy, Locke & Latham's goal-setting principles
  • Accessible and responsive: WCAG 2.1 AA compliance, works on any device
  • Certificate of completion with a personal Confidence Plan students can print and use

Development

Built as a single-file interactive web application using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. No external dependencies or subscriptions required. Progress tracking via localStorage. Rise-quality interactions without the $1,399/year price tag.

Results

A fully functional, standards-aligned interactive learning module ready for classroom deployment. Demonstrates proficiency in instructional design, learning science, interactive content development, and accessibility compliance.

Case Study: TitanPE FitnessGram Assessment Tracker

The Challenge

PE teachers administering FitnessGram fitness tests need to score students against age- and gender-specific Healthy Fitness Zone (HFZ) standards, track results across classes, and generate reports. Existing tools were either expensive, complex, or required internet access in a gym with no reliable WiFi.

Analysis

User Profile: K-8 PE teachers with limited technology time, often entering scores on a phone or tablet between classes.

Needs Assessment: Teachers need a tool that works offline, auto-calculates HFZ zones, handles multiple classes, and generates printable reports, all without training or setup.

Constraints: Must work on any device, no app install required, zero learning curve.

Design Decisions

  • Single HTML file: opens in any browser, works offline, no installation
  • Mobile-first interface: designed for phone/tablet use in a gym
  • Auto-scoring engine: built-in FitnessGram HFZ standards by age and gender
  • Quick Entry mode: rapid score input for class-sized groups
  • Roster import: paste from spreadsheet to add students instantly
  • Print-ready reports: individual student reports and class summaries

Iteration

v1.0: Core scoring and class management. Shipped to Teachers Pay Teachers marketplace.

v1.1: Fixed input sizing, improved Sit & Reach L/R handling, added roster import, optimized Quick Entry for no horizontal scrolling.

v1.2 (in development): Enhanced print reports, student flags and notes, achievement certificates.

Results

A real product used by real teachers, available on TPT, a marketplace serving 50,000+ PE educators. Demonstrates full product lifecycle: problem identification, user research, design, development, deployment, iteration based on feedback.

Case Study: K-5 Volleyball Unit

The Challenge

Design a multi-week volleyball unit for K-5 students that progresses from zero experience to game play, differentiates across wide ability ranges, and aligns to Illinois State Learning Standards.

Design Process

A
Analyze
D
Design
D
Develop
I
Implement
E
Evaluate

Analysis

Learner Profile: K-5 students across multiple schools, ages 5-11, wide range of motor development and prior experience.

Standards: IL State Goal 19 (motor skills), Goal 20 (fitness), Goal 21 (teamwork and cooperation).

Design: Scaffolded Skill Progression

  • Week 1: Underhand serving, individual wall practice with specific cues (step, hit with heel of hand, follow through)
  • Week 2: Bumping/Forearm pass, individual drills progressing to partner passing with measurable outcomes
  • Week 3: Setting/Overhand pass, technique with hoop targets for accuracy measurement
  • Week 4: Overhand serving, advanced skill with biomechanical cues
  • Week 5: Modified game play, applying all skills in game situations

Key Instructional Strategies

  • Explicit cue-based instruction: every skill broken into 3-5 observable cues
  • Scaffolded complexity: individual practice → partner drills → small group → game
  • Differentiation: beach balls/trainers for developing students, regulation balls for advanced
  • Assessment: teacher observation against cue performance, student self-assessment, peer feedback
  • SEL integration: reflection prompts, encouragement protocols, cooperation standards
  • Bellringers: cognitive warm-ups connecting to lesson content

Results

Delivered across 3 schools, K-5, with documented student progression from individual skill practice to cooperative game play. Unit demonstrates backward design, scaffolded instruction, standards alignment, differentiation, and formative assessment, all core instructional design competencies.

Case Study: FitnessGram Administration Training Guide

The Challenge

New PE teachers and substitute instructors often administer FitnessGram tests incorrectly. Inconsistent protocols lead to unreliable data. Design a training resource that ensures any teacher can administer tests correctly without one-on-one coaching.

Design Process

A
Analyze
D
Design
D
Develop
I
Implement
E
Evaluate

Analysis

Learner Profile: Adult professional learners: PE teachers, substitutes, paraprofessionals. Varying levels of FitnessGram familiarity. Need quick-reference format, not a manual.

Key Problems: Inconsistent test administration (especially PACER and Curl-Up cadence), incorrect scoring, confusion about age/gender HFZ thresholds.

Design Approach

  • Scenario-based learning: "A student reaches the line late on the PACER beep. What do you do?"
  • Quick-reference decision trees: for common judgment calls during testing
  • Visual protocols: step-by-step with correct form illustrations
  • Common mistakes section: what teachers get wrong most often and how to fix it
  • Self-check quiz: verify understanding before administering tests

Results

Demonstrates ability to design training materials for adult professional learners, a core requirement for corporate and K-12 instructional design roles. Combines domain expertise with learning design principles.
