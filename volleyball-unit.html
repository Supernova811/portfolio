Curriculum Design Artifact

K-5 Volleyball Unit

A Standards-Aligned, Scaffolded Skill Progression
Designer: Kevin Terranova, M.S. Grade Band: K-5 (differentiated) Duration: 5-6 Weeks Schools: 3 buildings, Rockford IL

Instructional Design Context

This unit was designed using backward design methodology (Wiggins & McTighe) and implemented across three schools serving K-5 populations with diverse motor skill levels. Each lesson follows a structured template: standards alignment, learning targets (affective, cognitive, psychomotor), bellringer, scaffolded procedures, differentiation, and formative assessment. The progression demonstrates how instructional scaffolding moves learners from zero experience to game application over 5-6 weeks.

Unit Overview

Design Philosophy

Start simple. Build success. Increase challenge. Confidence grows. Each skill is broken into 3-5 observable cues. Students master individual skills before combining them, and practice with modified equipment (balloons, beach balls, trainers) before regulation volleyballs.

Week Focus Skill Progression Assessment
1 Underhand Serve Individual wall practice → Hoop targets → Serve Wars game Cue observation (2 of 4 cues)
2 Bumping (Forearm Pass) Individual wall drills → Partner passing → Keep It Up Cue observation + partner feedback
3 Setting (Overhand Pass) Individual toss → Partner setting → Hoop target game Cue observation + accuracy count
4 Overhand Serve Wall practice → Hoop targets → Partner distance challenge Cue observation (2 of 4 cues)
5-6 Modified Game Play Skill review → Rotation & rules → Small-sided games Game performance + peer assessment

Standards Alignment

  • IL State Goal 19: Acquire movement and motor skills and understand concepts necessary to engage in moderate to vigorous physical activity (19.A.2a, 19.A.2b, 19.A.3a)
  • IL State Goal 20: Achieve and maintain a health-enhancing level of physical fitness based upon continual self-assessment
  • IL State Goal 21: Develop skills necessary to become a successful member of a team by working with others during physical activity (21.B.1a, 21.B.2a)
  • SHAPE America Standard 1: Demonstrates competency in a variety of motor skills and movement patterns
  • SHAPE America Standard 4: Exhibits responsible personal and social behavior that respects self and others

Lesson Plans

Week 1: Underhand Serve

Grades K-5 (Differentiated) ▼
Affective
Students will encourage their fellow classmates throughout the lesson, motivating them and correcting form
Cognitive
Students will identify the cues for the underhand serve in volleyball
Psychomotor
Students will demonstrate the skill cues for the underhand serve
Equipment
Cones, volleyballs (regulation + trainers), beach balls, balloons, hula hoops, bean bags
Warm-Up (5-7 min)

Relay Sprints: Squads line up behind cones. First student sprints down, touches the red line, sprints back and tags the next person. Repeat 2-3 times. Variation: leaping, backpedaling, skipping to incorporate locomotor skills.

Fitness Activity (6-10 min)

Tic Tac Toe: Teams stand behind a cone, sprint to hula hoops, place bean bags to form 3-in-a-row. Builds cardio endurance while reinforcing teamwork and quick decision-making. Groups of 6 (3 per team) or by squads.

Lesson Focus (20-25 min): Scaffolded Progression

Step 1:Demonstrate & Cue Introduction: Students sit on the black line. Teacher demonstrates underhand serve with explicit cues. Students echo and mirror each cue.

Step: Opposite foot forward Hit: Heel of hand Follow through: Toward target

Step 2:Individual Wall Practice: Students find space 10 feet from the wall. Using cues, serve the ball and try to hit above the red line. Track consecutive successes. K-2 students use balloons or beach balls; 3-5 use trainer volleyballs.

Step 3:Hoop Target Challenge: 6 groups under basketball hoops. Serve from the free throw line, trying to get the ball into the hoop. Count successes to build motivation through measurable progress.

Step 4:Serve Wars (if time): Class divides into 2 teams. One side serves at a time, aiming at colored mats. Points for hitting the target. Builds serving accuracy under game-like pressure.

Closure (5 min)

Review & Reflect: Students line up. Teacher asks: "What skill did we work on? Name one cue for the underhand serve. How many times did you hit above the red line? What do you need to work on?"

Differentiation

K-2: Balloons and beach balls; closer wall distance; focus on 2 of 3 cues. 3-5: Trainer volleyballs progressing to regulation; full cue set; accuracy targets.

Assessment

Teacher observation against cue performance. Outcome targets: "I can serve a volleyball to a wall demonstrating two of three cues." "I can underhand serve and hit the ball above the red line more than 5 times."

Week 2: Bumping (Forearm Pass)

Grades K-5 (Differentiated) ▼
Affective
Students will encourage and coach their peers throughout the bumping lesson
Cognitive
Students will demonstrate knowledge of the bump (forearm pass) used in volleyball
Psychomotor
Students will physically demonstrate the performance cues for the volleyball bump
Equipment
Cones, volleyballs (regulation + trainers), beach balls, balloons, hula hoops
Warm-Up (5-7 min)

Walk/Jog Intervals: Class split into 2 groups. One group jogs along court boundary lines while the other walks inside. Switch every 2 minutes. Incorporate locomotor variations (high knees, skipping, side shuffle). Builds cardio while discussing difference between jogging and sprinting.

Fitness Activity (6-10 min)

RPS Showdown: Teams line up, one student jumps through hula hoops across the board. When two students from opposing teams meet, they play Rock-Paper-Scissors. Winner continues; loser exits. First team to reach the other team's cone collects a token. Builds cardio, sportsmanship, and quick reaction.

Lesson Focus (20-25 min): Scaffolded Progression

Step 1:Technique Introduction: Demonstrate the forearm pass hand position: hands clasped, forearms parallel, thumbs together, wrists turned down, elbows locked. Show ready position: feet shoulder width, knees bent, arms out.

Wrists together Create flat platform Ready position: feet shoulder width, knees bent Move to ball Pass to target

Step 2:Individual Wall Drills: Stand 6 ft from wall. Pass the ball against the wall and catch it. Progress to: alternating overhand/forearm pass, moving side to side to reach the ball, passing directly overhead and catching.

Step 3:Partner Passing Drills: Partners 10-15 ft apart. Player A tosses to Player B who bumps it back. Progress to: continuous passing (keep ball in the air), stationary player + moving player, both players with hoops (keep one foot inside while passing).

Step 4:Hoop Target Game: Squads assigned to a basketball hoop. One person tosses from the poly spot, partner bumps/sets the ball trying to make it into the hoop. Rotate after each attempt. Track group totals.

Closure (5 min)

Review & Reflect: "What skill did we work on today? Name one cue for the bump. How many times did you get it into the hoop? What do you need to work on to be more successful?"

Differentiation

K-2: Balloons for all activities; focus on "keep it up" rather than wall drills; partner "Bullseye" into hoops on the ground. 3-5: Trainer volleyballs; full partner progression; accuracy targets at hoops.

Assessment

Teacher observation against cue performance. Outcome targets: "I can bump a ball in the air using a forearm pass demonstrating two of five cues." "I can bump a ball for my partner using the cues." "I can bump the ball into the hoop from different locations."

Week 3: Setting (Overhand Pass)

Grades K-5 (Differentiated) ▼
Affective
Students will encourage and coach their peers throughout the setting lesson
Cognitive
Students will demonstrate knowledge of the set (overhand pass) used in volleyball
Psychomotor
Students will physically demonstrate the performance cues for the volleyball set
Equipment
Cones, volleyballs (regulation + trainers), beach balls, balloons, hula hoops
Warm-Up (5-7 min)

Walk/Jog Intervals: Same format as Week 2. Incorporate locomotor variations to maintain engagement. Review bumping cues during walking intervals.

Fitness Activity (6-10 min)

Sharks and Minnows: One student (shark) stands in the middle. Minnows walk toward the shark until they shout "Shark attack!", then minnows sprint to the opposite boundary. Tagged minnows become sharks. Last 1-2 minnows become sharks for the next round. Builds agility, spatial awareness, and cardiovascular endurance.

Lesson Focus (20-25 min): Scaffolded Progression

Step 1:Technique Introduction: Demonstrate the overhand pass (set): hands form a cup shape above the forehead, thumbs and forefingers close together, other fingers spread. Contact the ball above eye level. Push with spread fingers, not palms. Straighten legs and follow through.

Hands up early Bowl-shaped hands (diamond with thumb/index) Catch and push to target Extend arms

Step 2:Individual Wall Drills: Stand 6 ft from wall. Set the ball against the wall and catch it. Progress to: alternating overhand pass with forearm pass, moving side to side, setting directly overhead (2 consecutive before catching).

Step 3:Partner Setting Drills: Partners 10-15 ft apart. Same progression as bumping week: controlled toss → set back, continuous setting, stationary + moving, hoop constraint.

Step 4:Hoop Target Game: Same format as Week 2 but using the set. Squads at basketball hoops, setting the ball from poly spots trying to make it into the hoop. Rotate and track group totals.

Closure (5 min)

Review & Reflect: "What skill did we work on today? What's the difference between a bump and a set? Name one cue for the set. How many times did you get it into the hoop?"

Differentiation

K-2: Balloons; focus on "catch position" to feel the hand shape before attempting live sets. 3-5: Trainer volleyballs; full partner progression; compare bump vs. set accuracy.

Assessment

Teacher observation against cue performance. Outcome targets: "I can set a ball in the air using an overhand pass demonstrating two of four cues." "I can set a ball for my partner." "I can set the ball into the hoop from different locations."

Week 4: Overhand Serve

Grades 2-5 ▼
Affective
Students will encourage their classmates, motivating them and providing specific feedback on form
Cognitive
Students will identify the cues for the overhand serve in volleyball
Psychomotor
Students will demonstrate the skill cues for the overhand serve
Equipment
Cones, volleyballs (regulation + trainers), hula hoops
Note
K-1 students continue practicing underhand serve and bumping during this week. Overhand serve is introduced for grades 2-5 only.
Warm-Up (5-7 min)

Relay Sprints: Squad-based relay format. Progression: sprint → backpedal → side shuffle → carioca.

Fitness Activity (6-10 min)

Tic Tac Toe: Same format as Week 1 to maintain familiarity and maximize activity time.

Lesson Focus (20-25 min): Scaffolded Progression

Step 1:Technique Introduction: Demonstrate the overhand serve. Player stands facing the net, left foot slightly forward, weight on the right (rear) foot. Ball held in the left hand across the body. Step forward, transfer weight, swing right arm back and forward to contact the ball just below center.

Step: Opposite foot forward Toss: 1-2 feet high Hit: Open hand Follow through: Snap wrist

Step 2:Wall Practice: 10 feet from the wall, overhand serve above the red line. Track consecutive successes. Compare to underhand serve. Which is more accurate?

Step 3:Hoop Target Challenge: 6 groups under basketball hoops. Overhand serve from the free throw line into the hoop. Count successes.

Step 4:Partner Distance Challenge: Partners face each other. After each successful overhand serve received, both take a step back. How far apart can you get while still making successful serves?

Closure (5 min)

Review & Reflect: "What's the difference between an underhand and overhand serve? Name one cue for the overhand serve. Which serve felt more natural? What do you want to practice more?"

Differentiation

Grades 2-3: Trainer volleyballs; closer distance; focus on 2 of 4 cues. Grades 4-5: Regulation volleyballs; full cue set; accuracy and distance targets. K-1: Continue underhand serve and bumping practice with balloons.

Assessment

Teacher observation against cue performance. Outcome targets: "I can overhand serve a volleyball to a wall demonstrating two of four cues." "I can overhand serve into the hoop." "I can explain the difference between the two serves."

Weeks 5-6: Modified Game Play

Grades 3-5 (K-2 Modified) ▼
Affective
Students will encourage and coach their peers during game play as well as demonstrate sportsmanship
Cognitive
Students will demonstrate the skills and knowledge used in the game of volleyball (serve, bump, set, rotation, scoring)
Psychomotor
Students will physically demonstrate the performance cues for the bump, set, and serve during game situations
Equipment
Cones, volleyballs, trainers, balloons, volleyball net, poly spots, hula hoops, bean bags
Warm-Up (5-7 min)

Walk/Jog Intervals with Skill Review: During walking intervals, teacher calls out cues and students demonstrate the hand positions for bump, set, underhand serve, and overhand serve.

Fitness Activity (6-10 min)

RPS Showdown or Tic Tac Toe: Student choice to build ownership. Both options maintain cardiovascular warm-up.

Lesson Focus (20-25 min): Game Application

Step 1:Skill Review with Balloon: Students sit criss-cross. Teacher reviews all skills with demonstration. Students each get a balloon and practice serving, bumping, and setting individually.

Step 2:Game Rules & Rotation: Demonstrate proper rotation, how to serve, the 3-hit rule, and scoring. Emphasize: every player rotates, every player serves, communication is required.

Step 3:Small-Sided Games: Pair up squads (8-10 per team, 6 on court). Remaining students scorekeep, referee, and cheer. Winner determined by RPS for first serve. Play to 5 points, then rotate losing team out for the waiting team.

K-2 Modification: Use balloons, lower net (or rope between chairs), allow catching and throwing as a bridge to bumping. Focus on "keep it in the air" rather than formal rules.

Closure (5 min)

Unit Reflection: "What was your favorite skill this unit? Which skill improved the most? What would you practice more? How did your team communicate during the game?"

Differentiation

K-2: Balloons, modified net height, catching/throwing allowed, cooperative "keep it up" format. 3-5: Trainer volleyballs, standard net, full rotation and scoring, competitive format with sportsmanship emphasis.

Assessment

Teacher observation of skill application in game situations. Peer assessment: "I can give positive feedback to my partner or group peers." Self-assessment: "I can assess myself and identify what I need to work on." Game performance: "I can perform the skill cues for bump/set/serve in the game of volleyball."

Instructional Design Rationale

Scaffolding Strategy

Every lesson follows the same progression: Demonstrate → Individual Practice → Partner Practice → Game Application. This mirrors the scaffolding principle from Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development: students work independently before adding the complexity of a partner, then a team.

Cue-Based Instruction

Each skill is broken into 3-5 explicit, observable cues. This gives students concrete targets to focus on and gives the teacher measurable criteria for formative assessment. Students don't need to perform perfectly; they need to demonstrate specific cues.

Differentiation by Design

Equipment modification (balloons → beach balls → trainers → regulation) is built into every lesson, not added as an afterthought. K-2 and 3-5 progressions run simultaneously with shared structure but different expectations.

Assessment Integration

Assessment is continuous, not summative-only. Every lesson includes teacher observation against cue performance, student self-assessment prompts during closure, and peer feedback during partner activities. Students know what "success" looks like before they begin.

SEL Integration

Affective objectives are equal to psychomotor and cognitive objectives. Every lesson includes encouragement protocols, cooperation standards, and reflection prompts connecting physical skill development to broader life skills.
